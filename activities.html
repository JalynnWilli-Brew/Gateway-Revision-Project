Sakura Festival


Cherry Blossom Festival, also known as Hanami or Sakura season, is a favorite to both locals and tourists! Cherry blossoms normally bloom in late March to mid-April or even in early May. Admiring these flowers is a tradition that goes back over a thousand years. Although, the first official festival began in 1935 and became an annual event in Japan.

Cherry blossoms are a symbolic flower of springtime, renewal, and the fleeting nature of life, as the life span of these flowers, is rather short. Celebrations may occur during the day or nighttime (the nighttime version is called yozakura) and are usually preferred as lights are strung up and the scenery is even more colorful.

There are a variety of locations to enjoy Hanami such as Ueno Park, Meguro River, and Takada Castle. At Ueno Park, families and friends picnic usually with homemade foods and drinks. You can also enjoy local crafts and food, trips on the boating lake, and enjoy music and colorful dance performances. At this park, there is also the famous "Last Samurai" statue of Saigo Takamori.

Free and for all ages!


Samurai Museum

The Samurai Museum in Tokyo is the site to see! In this museum, they give tours in which you will learn the rich history at at a much deeper level. You will learn how the samurai fought, lived, who they were and what they believed in.

They have a huge variety of displays that include authentic artifacts such as samurai helmets, weapons, and armor. There is also a sword show every day between 2pm and 5pm where you can watch two trained actors performing sword fighting.

They also have a photo booth in which you can either dress up as a samurai warrior or wear kimono outfits. There is also a museum shop worth visiting to find awesome gifts!

Admission Fee: Adults: $12.97 Children under 12: $6.49 Children under 3: free


Tokyo Skytree


The Tokyo Skytree is an observation and broadcasting tower in Sumida, Tokyo. The Skytree became the tallet structure in Japan in 2010 and is the tallest structure in the world. It is 634 meters tall and was completed on a leap year in 2012.

The Skytree gift shop is on level 345, the cafe is on level 340 and the Musashi Sky restauarant is on 345 with panoramic views of Tokyo. The tembo deck floor is on levels 340 and 350 while the tembo galleria is on level 445.

Floor 350 is covered in 5 meter high glass for a 360 degree panoramic view. The galleria will take you from floor 445 to skytree's highest floor 450 via a glass tube. This will leave you convinced you are walking on the sky!

Book tickets online and enjoy a cheaper price than same day tickets! All Ages!
